Life of Muhammad Ali Jinnah

  • 1876 - Muhammad Ali Jinnah is born on December 25 in Karachi, which was then part of British India.
  • 1892 - Jinnah leaves for London to study law at the age of 16.
  • 1896 - Jinnah is called to the bar in England, becoming a barrister.
  • 1913 - He joins the Indian National Congress, initially advocating for Hindu-Muslim unity.
  • 1916 - Jinnah briefly leaves politics due to disagreements with Congress leadership.
  • 1920 - Jinnah returns to politics and becomes a prominent leader in the All-India Muslim League.
  • 1940 - Jinnah delivers the historic Lahore Resolution, which calls for the creation of a separate Muslim state in British India.
  • 1947 - India gains independence from British rule, and Pakistan is created as a separate nation on August 14. Jinnah becomes Pakistan's first Governor-General.
  • 1948 - Muhammad Ali Jinnah becomes Pakistan's first President.
  • 1948 - Jinnah delivers his famous speech on Pakistan's future as a tolerant and democratic state.
  • 1948 - On September 11, Muhammad Ali Jinnah passes away in Karachi at the age of 71.

Ideologies and Thoughts:

  • Jinnah is widely regarded as the "Father of the Nation" in Pakistan for his pivotal role in the country's creation.
  • He was a strong advocate for the rights of Muslims in India and believed in the importance of a separate nation for Muslims to safeguard their interests.
  • Jinnah promoted religious tolerance and envisioned Pakistan as a democratic and inclusive state where all citizens would be equal regardless of their faith.
  • He emphasized the importance of constitutionalism, rule of law, and parliamentary democracy in the governance of Pakistan.
